The Reunion Venue

Omni Amelia Island Plantation Resort

The Omni Amelia Island Plantation Resort will be the home to the JSConf 5th year family reunion and is the perfect setting for an amazing time. Far more secluded than any previous JSConf, the resort has everything you might want and need to do all within walking (or segway, if you prefer) distance. Whether you fancy a geek golf tournament, bonfires on the beach, or just a little NodeCopter hackathon - there is something for everyone at the Resort.

The dates for this event line up perfectly with the Memorial Day US holiday (May 27th) and we encourage you to bring your family with you. The resort is a perfect family (or individual) vacation before/after/during the conference as the water and weather will be warm! Also bear in mind that many other JS developers will probably also be extending their stay, so you can have a full week of JSConf awesomeness!

How To Get Here!

Locate: Amelia Island

Nestled in Florida, just south of Georgia, the barrier island of Amelia Island is an absolute paradise, in part because it is remote location. The closest airport is Jacksonville International Airport (JAX), roughly 29 miles from the resort. We, JSConf, will be providing transportation between the airport and the resort on May 28th and June 1st, details to come shortly. If you opt to rent a car OR drive to Amelia Island, there is plenty of free parking on the resort If you do bring a car, you can head to the historic and beautiful Fernandina Beach. There will be shuttles to Fernandina as well, but only on a limited, pre-arranged basis.
See you on the island!

Why is it called the 5th year reunion?

Maybe this is your first year, maybe it is your fifth - either way if you are at JSConf, you are family! It is rare for a crazy social experiment like JSConf to last for 5 whole years and still be growing, changing, and making things better. But here we are! This year, we are revamping the entire conference model. The event will have four discrete tracks over two days (Wednesday and Friday) with an intermission day in-between (Thursday). The tracks are broken down as follows:

  • Track A - An organizer curated track with competing alumni versus new speakers.
  • Track B - A "first-come, first-served" track similar to an un-conference style where attendees are provided the chance to speak on any topic of their choice. Talking slots will be similar in length to the Track A (roughly 30 minutes). These speaking slots will open up to attendees right before the event.
  • Training Track - A dedicated track to educational aspects of JS in all facets.
  • Significant Others Track - A planned track for significant others of attendees that are interested in something other than JS. We are strongly encouraging attendees to bring their close family because people are more than just islands and we want that well represented.

A quick note on our new model. We are focusing on intimate small group discussions while simultaneously supporting a broader range of attendees. In order to do this, we are recreating our traditional "big parties" into family style dinners. The intermission day (Thursday) will be a break out to allow people to relax and make connections without the worry of "missing something amazing" during the conference. We are providing these small events as opportunities for amazing networking opportunities that are otherwise unheard of at technology events.
